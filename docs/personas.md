# Personas — Enough

The reusable cast for our persona-driven evaluation loop (see
[evaluation-process.md](./evaluation-process.md)). Three **user** personas judge the
*app*; two **lenses** judge the *strategy* and the *maker*.

> Personas are representative archetypes, not real individuals. They share one cohort —
> Indian-origin tech professionals who came up together — caught at three different
> moments of the same decision. P2 is inspired by a described classmate archetype,
> intentionally composite/anonymized.

**Shared context (the cohort).** All three graduated CS around 2014 from good Indian
universities, moved to the US for work or a master's, and are now ~9–13 years into tech
careers with real money and a quietly recurring question: *do we stay, move, or go home?*
What separates them is **how far along that decision they are** — and that's exactly what
the product must meet them at.

## User personas

### P1 · Arjun — the Builder-Optimizer *(primary; ≈ the maker, anonymized)*

**Snapshot.** 34. Born in Pune, CS undergrad 2014, MS in the US, ~9 years in. Lives in
the Bay Area (San Mateo). Senior PM (eng background) at a mid-size tech company; spouse
is a product designer. One kid, age 3. Household income ~$500k; liquid net worth ~$1.8M
(RSUs, index funds, a little crypto, a 401k he reads the fee disclosures on).

**Context & trigger.** A bad RSU-refresh cycle, a round of layoffs at a friend's company,
and his parents getting older have him asking the real question for the first time:
*"Are we actually free, or just running on a faster treadmill?"* He already keeps a
personal FIRE spreadsheet — but it's US-only, two years stale, and it can't answer the
India question that's actually on his mind.

**Goals.** A number he can **trust and take apart**. He wants to understand the genuine
trade-offs — FX, taxes, earning power that differs by country — and make a *rational*
call, not an emotional one. He'd rather see an honest range than a confident wrong number.

**Behaviors.** Reads the methodology before the result. Opens every ⓘ. Reconciles the
"show the math" line by line. Distrusts round numbers and false precision. Tries to break
the model (extreme SWR, zero net worth). Checks the sources and their dates. Would
genuinely fork the repo to read `assumptions.ts`.

**Pains / objections.** Black-box math ("says who?"). A single point estimate with no
error bars — to him that reads as *naïve*, not authoritative. Not being able to tweak the
assumptions he most doubts (FX, local prices). Anything that smells like a blog's
back-of-envelope guess.

**Job-to-be-done.** *"Give me a defensible, auditable 'enough' number across the places I'd
actually consider, so I can make a rational stay-or-go call — and let me stress-test it."*

**Wins him →** transparency + uncertainty (ranges) + control (editable assumptions) +
sourced, dated data. **Loses him →** any whiff of hand-waving.

**Representative quote.** *"I don't trust a number I can't take apart."*

**Strategic role.** The hardest to win and the most valuable. If he believes it, he shares
it into precisely the right network and lends it credibility. If he doesn't, he's the
sharpest critic. Design to *his* trust bar and everyone else clears it.

### P2 · Devang — the Settled Engineer *(the volume audience)*

**Snapshot.** 33. Arjun's CS classmate from Pune; stayed in pure engineering, now a Staff
Software Engineer. In Seattle on a green card; spouse works part-time; two kids (5 and 2).
Household income ~$350k; net worth ~$1.2M, most of it in retirement accounts and home
equity he hasn't really tallied.

**Context & trigger.** Arjun drops an Enough link in their college WhatsApp group: *"check
your number."* Devang has idly wondered "could we move back near my parents?" but never
actually modeled it — life is full with work and young kids. He opens the link **on his
phone, at 11pm, between a deploy and sleep.**

**Goals.** A **fast, credible gut-check**: *"is moving back even financially plausible?"* —
in about a minute, with zero homework. Ideally one clean line he can text his wife.

**Behaviors.** Mobile, impatient, skims. Won't read the controls; sets two or three things
and hunts for the takeaway. Bounces if it feels like a chore or assumes the wrong thing
about him (e.g., that he's retired). Forwards instantly if it gives him a "huh, *really?*"
moment. Will not create an account for this — ever.

**Pains / objections.** Too many inputs before any value. A default scenario that doesn't
look like *his* life. Jargon. Anything over ~2 minutes. Absolute numbers when what he
actually wants is the *difference* from his current life.

**Job-to-be-done.** *"In one glance on my phone, tell me whether moving back is in the realm
of possible — and hand me one line I can send my wife."*

**Wins him →** speed, a default that fits an employed person, the plain-English summary,
the stay-or-go delta ("vs Seattle…"), a card worth forwarding. **Loses him →** friction,
irrelevance, density.

**Representative quote.** *"Just tell me if this is crazy or not."*

**Strategic role.** The **largest slice of the TAM arrives like Devang** — via a shared
link, on a phone, with low patience. His engagement and re-share *are* the growth loop. If
the first screen doesn't feel like his life in 5 seconds, the loop leaks.

### P3 · Meera — the Determined Returner *(the highest-intent believer)*

**Snapshot.** 38. Grew up in Chennai; came to the US for an MS, stayed 13 years; now a
Director in tech. Husband is a professional too; two kids (8 and 5). Household income
~$420k; net worth ~$2.7M. **They've already decided: moving back to India within ~18
months** — for aging parents, for the kids to grow up near family, for a life that feels
like theirs.

**Context & trigger.** A recent trip home crystallized it; the kids are still young enough
to transplant; her parents won't be independent forever. The decision is *made*. Now it's
planning and reassurance — and she's tired of US-centric tools that don't even list her
cities.

**Goals.** *Where* in India fits their means (ideally Chennai or a nearby metro), *how much*
they need, and whether they're **ready now or how far off**. And — because this decision is
at least half-emotional — reassurance about the **non-financial** life: schools, air,
healthcare, proximity to family.

**Behaviors.** Explores the India options, wants to clear out the US noise, looks for
quality-of-life signals, and hunts for a "you're ready" milestone. Emotionally invested in
seeing *their* city in the list. Shares with her husband and her sister, not a WhatsApp
group.

**Pains / objections.** US cities cluttering a list she only wants for India. A cold,
money-only framing when her decision is half about life. Her hometown being missing (a
personalization cliff). No sense of "are we there yet."

**Job-to-be-done.** *"We've decided to move to India — show me which city we can afford,
whether we're ready, and reassure me this is a good life, not just a cheaper one."*

**Wins her →** India depth, the readiness frame ("funded by age X" / "already funded"),
life signals (air quality), and her city actually being present. **Loses her →** irrelevant
US clutter, cold output, a missing hometown.

**Representative quote.** *"We've decided — now help me feel sure."*

**Strategic role.** The highest-intent user and a natural evangelist within her family. She
reveals the roadmap: more sourced India cities, and lightweight life-quality signals beyond
money.

## Evaluation lenses (assess strategy & the maker)

### L1 · "Senior PM / Startup" *(your strategic lens)*
Treats Enough as a startup. Asks: what's the wedge and moat? The riskiest assumption?
Where's the growth loop and the retention reason? The MVP→PMF path, and what to cut?
Output = a direction recommendation to debate.

### L2 · "Principal PM evaluating the maker (Ravi)"
Assesses whether the work shows **principal-level** craft across problem framing, user
empathy & validation, prioritization, strategy (wedge/moat/distribution), metrics &
outcomes, execution, and communication. Output = a candid scorecard + the specific moves
to level up.
