# Personas — Enough

The reusable cast for our persona-driven evaluation loop (see
[evaluation-process.md](./evaluation-process.md)). Three **user** personas judge the
*app*; two **lenses** judge the *strategy* and the *maker*.

> Personas are representative archetypes, not real individuals. P2 is inspired by a
> described classmate archetype, intentionally composite/anonymized — we don't build
> personas from a named person's private profile.

---

## User personas — they assess value & usability

### P1 · "Arjun, the Builder-Optimizer" *(you, anonymized)*
- **Snapshot:** ~33, Indian-origin, 2014 Pune CS undergrad. Started in software, moved
  into product; now a product-minded builder. Financially literate, deeply analytical.
- **Situation:** personally weighing the stay-in-US vs return-to-India question; dual
  income, 1 kid, net worth in the low millions.
- **Cares about:** rigor, transparency, *why* the numbers are what they are; wants to
  stress-test assumptions (FX, prices, tax, returns) and trust the model before acting.
- **Fails the app if:** the numbers feel like a black box, or a simplification looks
  wrong enough to undermine trust.

### P2 · "Devang, the Settled Engineer" *(2014 Pune CS cohort archetype)*
- **Snapshot:** ~33, same graduating class, stayed in pure engineering ~11 years; now
  senior/staff. Busy, pragmatic, not a personal-finance hobbyist.
- **Situation:** still working; idly wondering "could I afford to move back?" Opens the
  link on his phone from a WhatsApp group.
- **Cares about:** a fast, credible gut-check. One clear answer. Minimal fiddling.
- **Fails the app if:** he's faced with 15 sliders before seeing value, or the defaults
  don't match someone who's still employed.

### P3 · "Meera, the Determined Returner"
- **Snapshot:** similar timeline & earning power, but has *already decided* to return to
  India (family, kids' grandparents, lifestyle).
- **Situation:** wants to know *where* in India, *how much*, and *are we ready yet*.
- **Cares about:** India depth, a sense of readiness/progress, and life beyond money
  (schools, air, family). US cities are noise to her.
- **Fails the app if:** the answer is buried under irrelevant US options, or it's purely
  a money number with no lived-life context.

## Lenses — they assess strategy & the maker

### L1 · "Senior PM / Startup" *(your strategic lens)*
Treats Enough as a startup. Asks: what's the wedge and moat? What's the riskiest
assumption? Where's the growth loop and the retention reason? What's the MVP→PMF path,
and what should we cut? Output = a direction recommendation to debate.

### L2 · "Principal PM evaluating the maker (Ravi)"
Assesses whether the work demonstrates **principal-level** PM craft across: problem
framing, user empathy & validation, prioritization, strategy (wedge/moat/distribution),
metrics & outcomes, execution craft, and communication/influence. Output = a candid
scorecard + the specific moves to level up.
