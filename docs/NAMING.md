# Naming — options & recommendation

The brief: the name should carry **"enough" + "fire"** (highest signal for the FIRE-aware
target audience) — *and* we want creative options that even people outside the
FIRE / "your enough number" in-group can understand.

## The core tension (and the solve)

A name does two jobs at once, and they pull in opposite directions:
1. **Signal** — make the in-group (FIRE-aware people) instantly feel "this is for me."
2. **Clarity** — make an outsider understand what it does.

One word rarely nails both. The professional solve is **name (signal) + tagline
(clarity)**: a brandable, in-group name does the winking; a plain-English tagline explains
it to everyone. So we don't have to choose — we pick a signal name and let the tagline
carry comprehension.

## Tier A — "enough" + "fire" (your brief: max in-group signal)

- **FIREnough** ⭐ — a portmanteau of **FIRE** + **enough**. Reads as "fire-enough," and
  puns on **"fair enough"** — a warm, conversational phrase that softens the jargon for
  outsiders. Hits both target words; memorable; brandable. Best of the set.
- **EnoughFIRE** — both words, plainly stated. Clearer, less clever; reads a little like a
  category label than a brand.
- **My FIRE Number / FIREnumber** — pairs FIRE with the "number" concept (the thing we
  compute). Very descriptive *to insiders*; flat to outsiders.
- **EnoughFI** — "FI" (financial independence) without the "RE" (retire early). Broader
  than FIRE — speaks to people who want *options*, not necessarily early retirement.

## Tier B — beyond the jargon (so outsiders get it)

These trade in-group signal for plain comprehension:
- **Freedom Number** — "freedom" is the mainstream-friendly synonym for "enough number";
  warm, aspirational, understood by anyone. (Trades away the FIRE wink.)
- **Work-Optional** — the friendlier rebrand of FIRE ("when does work become optional?").
  Self-explaining and aging better than "FIRE."
- **Retire Where? / RetireWhere** — names the actual cross-border twist (not just *whether*,
  but *where*). Instantly communicates the unique angle.
- **Enough, Anywhere** — keeps "enough," adds the cross-border promise, reads as plain
  English.

## Tier C — the hybrid (recommended shape)

Signal name + plain tagline, e.g.:

> **FIREnough** — *Your enough number: how much you need to stop working, in every city
> you'd actually live.*

In-group reads "FIRE + enough"; everyone else reads the tagline and gets it immediately.

## Recommendation

- **If max FIRE signal is the goal (your stated brief): `FIREnough`.** It's the rare name
  that lands the in-group ("FIRE" + "enough") *and* carries a friendly double meaning
  ("fair enough") so it doesn't fully alienate outsiders — and the tagline closes the gap.
- **If widest comprehension is the goal: `Freedom Number`** (or "Enough, Anywhere").
- **My pick: `FIREnough`** with a plain tagline doing the outsider work.

**Honest caveat:** "FIRE" is a slightly insider, somewhat dated term — some find it cringe,
and "freedom number" / "work-optional" are aging better with a general audience. But the
brief asks for maximum signal to the FIRE crowd, and **FIREnough** delivers that while the
"fair enough" read and the tagline keep it accessible.
